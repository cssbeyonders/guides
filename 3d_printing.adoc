[#nozzle-and-printer-selection]
=== Nozzle and Printer Selection

You can change the nozzle size and printer in the top left corner.

NOTE: The nozzle size is the diameter of the extruded filament. A smaller number results in a finer object printed.

TIP: It is found out that a smaller nozzle size can also reduce the weight due to reduced wall thickness. Therefore, 0.4 nozzle is better for most situations.

.Selecting printer and nozzle size.
image::printer_nozzle.png[Printer and Nozzle Selection, 600, 400]

. If the list does not have the nozzle and printer you want:
.. Click the *Select/Remove printers*.
.. Check the printers you want to add
.. Click the *+* button for nozzle.
.. Finally, click *Confirm* to save the changes.

+
.The printer list.
image::printer_nozzle_add.png[Printer List, 600, 400]

+
[TIP]
====
[disc]
- You can also view the size limit of the printers in the  printer list.
- After selecting a printer and nozzle, the plate will change according to the printer model.
- You might want to reposition your model when the plate changes.
====

. Select the printer and nozzle size that you want to use from the list.

[#multicolor-and-filament-selection]
=== Multicolor and Filament Selection

You can change or add filament type using the *Filament* window on the top right corner.

IMPORTANT: You cannot set up filament type, multicolor, or printing temperature on the printer; *you must set it up in the software.*

.The filament window.
image::multifilament.png[Filament Window, 900, 600]

. Add a new filament by pressing *+*.
. Modify the filament's type by pressing the *down arrow* next to the type.
. Change the filament's represented color by pressing the *number* and selecting a color.

+
IMPORTANT: Changing the color here has nothing to do with the actual printed color. It is only the represented color in the software.

. Next, use the *Color painting* tool to color your model.

+
.The location of the "Color Painting" tool on the toolbar.
image::fill_tool.png[Color Painting Tool, 400, 100]

+
.The UI of the "Color Painting" tool.
image::paint_popup.png[Color Painting UI, 400, 600]

. After filling multiple colors, a prime tower is needed. Make sure your model does not collide with the prime tower.

+
.Prime Tower.
image::prime_tower.png[Prime Tower, 600, 400]

+
However, our printer only supports 1 filament at the same time. Filling the surfaces has no effect on the actual color printed. Therefore, we need to <<adding-pauses, add a pause>> during the print and change the filament during the pause.

IMPORTANT: <<slicing, Slice>> the place first before adding a pause.

NOTE: Filling the color makes it easier to identify parts.

[#filament-types]
=== Filament Types

There are a few types of filaments. The most common ones are *PLA* and *ABS*. They are used for different purposes.

.PLA and ABS Comparison
|===
|Feature |PLA |ABS

|Printing Temperature
|Lower
|Higher

|Printing Requirements and Warping
|Few. Less prone to warping.
|More. Requires heated bed and enclosure to prevent warping.

|Strength
|Moderate
|High

|Toxicity and Odor
|Low odor and non-toxic.
|Strong plastic odors and produce toxic fumes. Requires good ventilation.

|Smoothening
|-
|With acetone

|Suitable for...
|Prototypes, parts with low stress requirements
|Parts requiring high strength, mechanical parts

|===

You can identify the type and color of a filament roll by reading its label.

.Label on filament. As we can see, it is PLA and is of color Natural.
image::filament_type_label.png[Label on Filament Roll, 400, 600]

[#changing-filament]
=== Changing Filament

. Go to *Control* > *Extrude/Retract* > *Retract*.

. Unlock the lock.

. Hold down the white part and pull the tube out.

. From the rack, pull the filament. While pulling, check if the filament on the nozzle side also gets pulled along.

+
If not, that means there are broken segments of filament within the tube. Push the filament instead of pulling. The segments should drop out from the nozzle side. After every segment drop, pull again to check if there are any remaining segments.

. Pull the filament all the way out and take away the filament roll.

. Place a new filament roll onto the rack and insert it into the tube.

+
IMPORTANT: Make sure that the filament and the tube goes through the hole on the rack.

. After it has reached the nozzle side, use a pair of pliers to cut the end such that it is tipped

+
TIP: This can make it easier to reach the bottom of the nozzle.

. Hold down the white part. Insert *only the filament* into the nozzle and to the very bottom.

. Go to *Control* > *Extrude/Retract* > *Extrude*. While extruding, push down the filament at the nozzle.

. If the extrusion is successful, hold down the white part and insert the tube.

. *Lock the lock.*

+
IMPORTANT: Locking the lock is very important. Otherwise, the nozzle will not be intaking.

[#starting-a-print]
=== Starting a Print

. Before doing anything, clean the board:
- Any remaining parts of the previous print
- Nozzle cleaning wastes
- Initial line

+
.Locations to Check for Cleaning the Plate.
image::plate_cleaning.png[Plate Cleaning Locations, 600, 400]

. Go to *Control* > *Extrude/Retract* > *Extrude* and test if the filament can extrude well. If not, <<could-not-print-out-filament, fix the problem with these steps>>.

. If you have loaded the file to the printer, go to *File* > select your model. Check if the filament type matches the filament type inserted. If yes, click *Print*.

+
If you have not loaded the file to the printer, <<connection-and-printing, follow these steps>>. Make sure that the selected filament type in the software matches the filament inserted.

[#taking-out-the-product]
=== Taking Out the Product

. If the printer has just finished printing, on the screen, go to *Control* > *Cooling*, then turn all the fans to the highest.

+
CAUTION: *Do not touch* anything right after it has finished printing. It is very hot.

. Detach the plate from the magnetic board using the grabbers.

+
.Grabbers on the Plate
image::plate_grabbers.jpg[Plate Grabbers, 600, 400]

. Bend the plate. The product will detach from the plate and you can remove it easily.

. To attach the plate back to the magnetic board, place the plate on it and push it in. At the bottom, *match the notches on the plate with the extrusions on the magnetic board.*

+
.Notches and Extrusions
image::notch_extrusion.png[Notches and Extrusions, 600, 400]

[#filament-ran-out]
=== Filament Ran Out

. There should be a pop-up on the screen. Click *Refill*.
. Follow <<changing-filament, the steps to change filament>>, *including the extrude test.*

+
[TIP]
====
Use the scrapers to separate the nozzle with the partially printed model to prevent the extruding filament from touching it.

.Scrapers.
image::scrapers.jpeg[Scrapers, 400, 600]
====

. On the screen, *resume printing*.

[#could-not-print-out-filament]
=== Could not Print Out Filament

The primary reason is that the supply of filament was interrupted but the printer could not detect the running out/blocking of filament supply.

There are many reasons this could happen. 

.Causes for Interruption of Filament Supply
[cols="a,a,a"]
|===
|Possible Causes |Solutions |Remarks

|Lock is locked.
|Unlock the lock.
|

|Filament not fully inserted.
|Push down the filament to the *very* bottom.
|There are multiple "layers" within the nozzle. What you thought was the bottom may not be the real bottom.

|Filament ran out.
|<<changing-filament, Change filament.>>
|

|Broken pieces of filament in tube.
|<<changing-filament, Remove broken pieces.>>
|
[disc]
- If the filament is easy to break, and there are tight turns in the tube, it is easy for this to happen.
- Filament that are not stored in proper environment is easier to break.

|There are filaments of other types stuck in the nozzle.
|
. On the screen, go to *Control* > *Extrude/Retract*.
. Configure it to a high temperature (like 260ºC).
. Press *Extrude*.
. While it is extruding, push down new filament into the nozzle.
. Take note of the extruded filament. If there are filament of other colors, repeat extruding until it is fully gone.
|This is because filaments like ABS require a higher temperature to melt. When we extrude using a higher temperature, it can melt the stuck filament.

|The filament tied in the filament roll.
|Untie/cut the filament.
|

|The temperature is not suitable for the filament in use.
|Go back to the software and <<multicolor-and-filament-selection, configure the filament to be correct>>.
|

|===

== Glossary
Extrusion::
. [In Fusion] The action of turning a 2D sketch into a 3D object by linear action.
. [In 3D Printer, Action] The action of ejecting filament from the nozzle.
. [In 3D Printer, Physical Object] The metal part at the back of the magnetic board for fixing the location of the plate.
Filament:: The material for 3D printing. It comes in rolls.
Grabber:: One of the two spots on the plate for you to grab and remove the plate.
Magnetic board:: An object in a 3D printer where the plate is fixed on.
Notch:: The indented part at the back of the plate where the extrusion is fitted.
Nozzle:: The apparatus in a 3D printer which intakes filament, heats it up, and extrudes it.
Plate:: The board in a 3D printer where 3D printing is done on.
Rack:: The place where filament rolls are stored.
Screen:: The display on the 3D printer.
Slice:: The action of turning a 3D model into layers for the 3D printer to print.
Support:: Extra printed filament for avoid hanging parts being printed in the air.
Tube:: The white plastic flexible cylinder connecting the rack and the nozzle of the printer to guide filament
